Legal Encyclopedia

Empower yourself with knowledge. Browse through key Indian laws, fundamental rights, and citizen safeguards.

Constitution

Right to Equality (Art 14)

The State shall not deny to any person equality before the law or the equal protection of the laws within the territory of India.

Constitution

Freedom of Speech (Art 19)

Protection of certain rights regarding freedom of speech, expression, assembly, association, movement, and residence.

Constitution

Protection of Life (Art 21)

No person shall be deprived of his life or personal liberty except according to procedure established by law.

Constitution

Right against Exploitation (Art 23-24)

Prohibits human trafficking, forced labor, and employment of children under 14 years of age in hazardous jobs.

Police Procedure

Rights upon Arrest (Art 22)

Right to be informed of grounds of arrest, right to consult a lawyer, and right to be produced before a magistrate within 24 hours.

Police Procedure

Zero FIR

An FIR can be filed in any police station (irrespective of jurisdiction) for cognizable offenses. Police must transfer it later.

Criminal Law

Theft (BNS Sec 303)

Moving a person's property without their consent is theft. Replaces IPC Section 379.

Women's Rights

Domestic Violence Act

Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005 provides civil remedies (protection orders, residence orders) for women facing abuse at home.

Women's Rights

POSH Act

Prevention of Sexual Harassment at Workplace Act, 2013 mandates an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) in organizations with 10+ employees.

Women's Rights

Maternity Benefit

The Maternity Benefit Act guarantees 26 weeks of paid leave for pregnant women employees in establishments with 10+ workers.

Women's Rights

No Night Arrest

According to CrPC/BNSS, women generally cannot be arrested after sunset and before sunrise, except in exceptional circumstances with Magistrate permission.

Consumer

Consumer Protection Act

Right to safety, right to be informed, right to choose, and right to seek redressal against unfair trade practices.

Consumer

MRP Rights

Consumers cannot be charged more than the Maximum Retail Price (MRP). You can bargain for less than MRP, but paying more is a violation.

Information

RTI Act 2005

Right to Information empowers citizens to demand information from any public authority, promoting transparency and accountability.

Digital

Cyber Stalking

Monitoring a person's internet usage or electronic communication to harass them is a punishable offense under the IT Act/BNS.
